# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Lost Then Found is used by PVHS students, staff, and administrators during normal school-day operations. Students need to quickly search for a found item, report something lost, or submit an item they found. Staff and administrators need a dependable moderation workspace for reviewing submissions, claims, lost reports, and audit activity.

## Product Purpose

The product exists to make the PVHS lost-and-found process searchable, accountable, and easier to complete. Success means a student can understand the correct first action immediately, item records stay clear enough for owners to recognize them, private pickup details remain restricted, and admins can process claims and records without confusion.

## Brand Personality

Calm, official, and helpful. The interface should feel like a school front desk with the clarity of Apple Support and the utility of a clean internal dashboard: practical, trustworthy, modern, and direct.

## Anti-references

Do not make the app feel like a generic AI SaaS homepage, a flashy startup landing page, or a decorative demo. Avoid glowing gradients, glassmorphism, ornamental shaders, over-large hero sections, busy card grids, and copy that sounds like marketing rather than school operations.

## Design Principles

Lead with the first task. Search comes first, with lost and found reports available as clear secondary paths.

Keep operations calm. Use restrained color, predictable controls, clear spacing, and quiet hierarchy so students and staff can act quickly.

Preserve trust. Make moderation, privacy, claim review, and admin-only information feel explicit and well managed.

Use familiar affordances. Standard buttons, forms, lists, tabs, and dashboards should behave the way users expect.

Design for repeated use. Admin and student dashboards should prioritize scanning, comparison, and action over decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Maintain the app's accessibility support, including HashRouter route announcements, skip link behavior, keyboard focus visibility, Radix-based controls, reduced-motion behavior, dark mode, high contrast mode, and dyslexic reading mode. Body text, form controls, placeholders, focus rings, and state colors should meet WCAG AA contrast expectations.
